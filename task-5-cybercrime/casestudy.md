Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

I want to tell you about Rahul, a college student who got cheated when he tried to sell his smartphone online. Someone contacted him after he posted an ad.
This person seemed like a real buyer. He agreed to the price away and wanted to pay using UPI immediately.. 
Instead of sending money he sent a link that said "collect request".

The cheating happened one step at a time. It was very tricky. First the cheat was very polite and seemed to be in a hurry so Rahul trusted him.
Then he sent a payment request. Told Rahul to accept it. Rahul thought he was getting money so he entered his UPI PIN.. 
Really he was allowing money to be taken out of his account.
The cheat did this again saying there was a problem and Rahul approved it times.

This kind of cheating usually happens to students, people who sell things online and those who do not know much about payments. 
It can be very bad with people losing money away feeling stressed and not trusting online payments anymore. 
Rahul lost ₹15,000 in a few minutes and it was hard to get the money back even after he told the bank.

This story shows how important it is to be careful. People need to remember that when they enter their UPI PIN it means money is being sent not received.
They should never say yes to payment requests, from people they do not know. UPI fraud is a problem and people need to be aware of UPI fraud to avoid it.
UPI fraud can happen to anyone so we need to be careful when using UPI.
