# Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students 

### General Safety Tips
Use strong, unique passwords for each account and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.  
Keep your apps, phone, and laptop updated to avoid security vulnerabilities.  
Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities like banking or logging into important accounts.  
Think before clicking—don’t open suspicious links or attachments from unknown emails, messages, or social media.  
Limit personal information shared online (college ID, address, phone number, etc.).  
Download apps only from trusted sources like the Google Play Store or Apple App Store.  
Regularly review and adjust your privacy settings on social media platforms.  
Log out from accounts when using shared or public devices (library, cyber café, etc.).  

### Financial / UPI Safety Tips  
Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details with anyone—even if they claim to be from a bank.  
Remember: Entering a UPI PIN means money is being sent, not received.  
Double-check payment requests—don’t approve unknown “collect requests.”  
Use official apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm and avoid third-party links.  

### Reporting Cybercrime  
Report incidents immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in  
Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 for urgent financial fraud cases
